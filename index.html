EVELYN HUGO

A Journey from the Rose Garden to Madison Square

From the book, The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo written by Taylor Jenkins Reid

Evelyn Hugo, iconic actress and trailblazer of Old Hollywood.

Early Life

Born Evelyn Elena Herrera in Hell’s Kitchen, New York, Evelyn grew up in a struggling Cuban-American family. Her early life was marked by hardship and ambition, as she dreamed of escaping poverty and achieving stardom, despite the discrimination she faced for her heritage.

Rise to Stardom

Evelyn’s journey to fame began when she left New York for Los Angeles in her teens, determined to make it in Hollywood. She rose to prominence in the 1950s, captivating audiences with her beauty and undeniable talent. Through calculated decisions and sheer determination, she became one of the most celebrated actresses of her time.

Legacy

Evelyn Hugo is remembered not only as a Hollywood legend but also as a trailblazer who redefined fame and power. Her story, revealed in her memoir, showcases a woman who navigated love, identity, and sacrifice while leaving an indelible mark on cinema and society.

Relationship with
Celia St. James

Celia St. James was the love of Evelyn Hugo’s life. Despite the societal pressures and personal challenges that kept them apart, their connection was undeniable and enduring. Evelyn and Celia shared a deep, passionate bond built on love, understanding, and forgiveness. Their relationship spanned decades of secrecy and sacrifice, with Evelyn ultimately prioritizing Celia’s happiness over societal expectations. Evelyn’s greatest regret was the time they lost, but their love remains the most defining part of her story.

Awards and Achievements

Academy Awards

Winner of two Oscars, including Best Actress for All for Us (1963) and Boute-en-Train (1974).

Box Office Queen

Starred in 20 films that broke box office records over her career.

Golden Globes

Recipient of five awards, including Best Actress for Little Women (1959).

Trailblazer Award

Recognized posthumously for breaking barriers for women and minorities in Hollywood.
